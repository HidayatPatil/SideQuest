    VISION

    SideQuest — where solo plans become shared experiences. From a quick shopping trip, a impromptu hike, or a concert you don’t want to go alone. There’s always someone to join you on your quest.

    MISSION

    Our purpose of connecting people again in the real world. Sharing everyday moments and meeting new people through spontaneous expeditions. More of our lives are moving online, we wanted to take a break from the main story and bring back the fun with adventures.

    VALUES

    Connection Over Perfection We care more about showing up than showing off.

    Our Logo

    Our logo features our charming mascots — a clever fox and a cute derpy frog. Our mascots appear frequently throughout our app and symbolizes friendship and adventure. By using mascots in our logo, we also hope to portray our app as fun and welcoming.

    Our wordmark uses the Darumadrop One font, which is inspired by Japanese handwriting combined with the classic Japanese game "Daruma Otoshi". We chose this font because it gives a playful and child-like vibe, perfectly fitting the overall theme of our app.

    Typography

    We chose Rubik for its clean, modern look that balances playfulness with readability. Its rounded, friendly design reflects the approachable and fun nature of our app, while maintaining clarity for users on their journey through quests.

    Why Everyday Adventure Matter?

    The Power of Shared Experiences

    Section 1 - Rubik

    We chose Rubik for its clean, modern look that balances playfulness with readability. Its sans-serif, rounded yet bold design reflects the approachable and fun nature of our app, while still maintaining clarity and ease of use for users on their journey through quests. Rubik’s versatility makes it an ideal choice for digital experiences that aim to be both engaging and accessible.

    Section 2 - OpenDyslexic

    OpenDyslexic is a free, open-source typeface designed to improve readability for individuals with dyslexia. With features like a weighted bottom and uniquely shaped letters, it enhances readability and reduces letter confusion. Its thoughtful design makes it helpful not only for users with dyslexia, but also for those with ADHD or other reading difficulties.

    Color Palette

    Our color palette balances playful energy with functionality, designed to make users feel welcome, excited, and ready to explore. It’s bright, accessible, and full of personality, drawn directly from our mascots, evoking a sense of bold adventure and curiosity.

    We chose blue for our primary color, as it’s easier to see compared to colors like orange or green. It is universally appealing and commonly associated with trustworthiness, stability, and a sense of community. Blue also offers strong contrast on digital interfaces, which enhances readability and accessibility for all users. This choice supports an inclusive design that prioritizes both visual comfort and clarity.

    Visuals

    Our visuals are all about creating a modern, approachable, and playful experience. Every element is designed with soft, rounded edges and plenty of breathing room to make the app feel inviting and easy to navigate.

    Graphics

    Many of our app's graphics feature our mascots Scout the fox and Goobert the frog, as well as their friends. The illustrations are first hand-sketched, then polished into vector drawings. They are colorful and fun, each made to feel like a scene from an RPG. Whether it’s onboarding screens, or our logo, the graphics invite users into a world where even a coffee run can feel like a grand adventure.

    Our playful mascot duo are inspired by fairytales and adventure stories. In particular, Scout the fox takes inspiration from the story “The Little Prince”, where the fox teaches the prince the importance of creating meaningful connections, understanding and appreciating others, and investing time in relationships. Our fox and his friend are the perfect representations of the message and vision we wish to share with SideQuest.
